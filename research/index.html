Taylor Dispersion in Capillaries with Triangular Cross-Sections

With: Dr. Francesca Bernardi, Professor Rich McLaughlin, and Professor Roberto Camassa

In this project we investigate the effect of triangular cross-sectional geometries on the evolution of a passive tracer injected in a capillary subject to laminar background flow. The role of the cross-sectional geometry in determining the skewness of the tracer concentration distribution at short and long times is of particular interest, as skewness is a measure of the asymmetries of the distribution. Over the course of this project, we have set up and conducted an experiment to gather data on the evolution of a passive tracer injected in an equilateral triangle capillary and compare the tracer distribution to numerical simulations. We have also developed theoretical methods to solve a Poisson's problem for Stokes flow in an equilateral capillary using complex variables techniques. Additionally, we have extended our work to isosceles triangle geometries and numerically solved Poisson's problem using the finite elements method in varying isosceles triangle domains. These results have showed a change in the loading behavior of a passive tracer in capillaries with isosceles triangle cross-sections, corresponding to a sign-change in the skewness from positive to negative. We are currently preparing a manuscript for submission to Physical Review Fluids .
